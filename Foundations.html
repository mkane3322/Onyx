History

The history of coding can be traced all the way back to the early 19th Century with Charles Babbage. In the 1820s the English mathematician created the Difference Machine. This machine was an automated mechanical calculator that was a major breajthrough in technology at the time. This invention would be expounded upon in 1843 by Ada Lovelace. She used the machine for general application coming to the realization that numbers can be a tool for everything. These two pinoneers are the two pinoneers of what we know today as coding.

When we jump the 20th century we see the majorities of the foundations laid for the modern programming environment we find in the current day. To start with the some of the early findings of Alan Turing. He is most known for cracking the German Engima code during World War II, but he has considerable contributions to the fiend of Computer Science itself. This would begin with his idea of a "universal machine" that could run programs that he would actualize into the first computer powered by electricity. This would lead to the first programming language being created in the 1940s by Konrad Zuse with Plankalkul. This language was very crude and was mainly used to preform repetitive tasks efficiently. This would get the ball rolling during the 20th century with some of the languages we see today like Pythan and C++. This would all culminate in 1995 with the creation of Java, Javascript, and PHP.

The introduction of said languages led to what is probably the most influential time for programmming in the dot com boom. This time period saw the introduction of the World Wide Web being available to the public at large. With the web progressivley becoming a norm in many households more resources and learning tools became prevelant. This culmination of broad accessability and rescources allowed for the wave of Computer Science we are familiar with today.
